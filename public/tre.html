What it is

A Contractor's Game Plan — Not a Surprise.

The TRE (Transaction Readiness) Report is a visual, non-invasive walkthrough by a licensed general contractor. We identify the correctable items most likely to come up at a buyer's inspection, rank each one by how much it threatens the sale, and price it — so your seller fixes what matters, on their terms, before a buyer ever uses it as leverage.

This is NOT a home inspection. It is a contractor's repair-planning advisory. It is not performed by a licensed home inspector, involves no destructive testing, and does not certify code compliance or warrant concealed conditions (Indiana IC 25-20.2). It complements — it does not replace — a licensed home inspection.
The difference

Inspection vs. TRE.

Both have a place. They answer different questions.

A home inspection

  • Ordered by the buyer, after an offer
  • Documents condition — a long list of findings
  • Doesn't tell you what to fix first, or what it costs
  • Becomes the buyer's negotiating leverage

The TRE Readiness Report

  • Done for the seller, before listing
  • Ranks items by what threatens a clean closing
  • Prices every item, tiered must-fix to value-add
  • Puts the seller in control of the repair conversation
How it works

Walk. Score. Rank. Price.

1

We walk the home

A non-invasive, room-by-room and exterior walkthrough — the same items an FHA or conventional appraiser and a buyer's inspector tend to flag.

2

We score every item

Each finding gets a closing-risk score (will it delay or block the deal / safety) and a buyer-appeal score (effect on offers and days on market).

3

We rank & tier it

Items sort highest-priority first and fall into three tiers — must-fix, recommended, and value-add — with FHA vs. conventional lending notes.

4

We price it

Every line gets a real price range and a tier subtotal, plus a full-program total — so there's a clear number, not a vague "you should fix that."

The tiers

What to Fix, in Order.

Tier 1

Must-fix

Lending and safety items that can delay or block an FHA/conventional appraisal — the things that threaten the closing itself.

Tier 2

Strongly recommended

Items a buyer's inspection will almost certainly surface and use as leverage. Address them and you control the negotiation.

Tier 3

Value-add

Optional improvements that strengthen offers, curb appeal, and time-on-market when the budget allows.

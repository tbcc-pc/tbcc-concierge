What it is

A Contractor's Game Plan — Not a Surprise.

The TRE (Transaction Readiness) Report is a general contractor's repair plan and estimate. We walk your home, recommend the repairs and improvements worth making before you list, put them in priority order by what matters most to buyers and lenders, and price each one — the same way we'd scope and quote any project. It's a game plan for getting the home market-ready, not an evaluation of its condition.

This is NOT a home inspection. It's a contractor's repair proposal and estimate — the work we'd recommend and what it costs — not a professional opinion on the condition of your home's systems. It is not performed by a licensed home inspector, involves no testing, and does not certify code compliance (Indiana IC 25-20.2). It does not replace a licensed home inspection.
The difference

Inspection vs. TRE.

Both have a place. They answer different questions.

A home inspection

  • Ordered by the buyer, after an offer
  • Documents condition — a long list of findings
  • Doesn't tell you what to fix first, or what it costs
  • Becomes the buyer's negotiating leverage

The TRE Readiness Report

  • Built for the seller, before you list
  • A prioritized plan of recommended repairs
  • Every item priced — must-fix to value-add
  • Work we can do and stand behind
How it works

Walk. Plan. Prioritize. Price.

1

We walk the home with you

A pre-listing walkthrough with your contractor — room by room and outside — the same way we'd look at any home before quoting work.

2

We build the repair plan

We list the repairs and improvements worth making before you list, and note which ones tend to matter to FHA/conventional lenders and buyers.

3

We prioritize the work

The plan is ordered into three tiers — must-fix, recommended, and value-add — so you know what to tackle first.

4

We price it

Every line gets a real price range plus tier subtotals and a total — a clear estimate, not a vague "you should fix that."

The tiers

What to Fix, in Order.

Tier 1

Must-fix

Repairs tied to lending or safety that can hold up an FHA/conventional appraisal — the work to handle first.

Tier 2

Strongly recommended

Repairs buyers commonly ask for — handle them up front and you keep control of the negotiation.

Tier 3

Value-add

Optional improvements that strengthen offers, curb appeal, and time-on-market when the budget allows.
